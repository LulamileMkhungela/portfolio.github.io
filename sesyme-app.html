Sesyme Educational Platform UX/UI Design

Democratizing Education Through Innovation

Sesyme is an innovative e-textbook subscription platform designed to make higher education affordable and accessible for South African university students. Born from the personal struggles of students who couldn't afford textbooks, this platform creates a collaborative learning ecosystem that connects students, graduates, and lecturers across multiple institutions.

Executive Summary

Market Challenge: 68% of SA students struggle with textbook costs
Average Textbook Cost: R2,500-R4,000 per subject
Student Debt Crisis: R35 billion in outstanding NSFAS debt
Solution Impact: 75% cost reduction for students
Knowledge Sharing: Cross-institutional collaboration
Safety Features: Anti-plagiarism and cyberbullying protection
Founder Vision: Walter Sisulu University alumni
Target Market: 1.1 million SA university students
Future Goal: Fourth Industrial Revolution readiness
Founded by Student Experience

"Being recent university students ourselves, these are one of the few things we had difficulties dealing with – the lack of socially shared knowledge between students, graduates and lecturers." - Silindile Ngwane, Co-founder

STEP 1: DEFINE

Identifying the educational accessibility crisis in South African higher education

PROBLEM STATEMENT

"How might we make quality educational resources affordable and accessible to financially disadvantaged South African university students while fostering collaborative learning across institutions?"

Market Reality

Student Population: 1.1 million university students
Textbook Crisis: 68% cannot afford required textbooks
Average Cost: R15,000-R20,000 per year in textbooks
NSFAS Gap: Allowances don't cover all academic materials

Success Metrics

Cost Reduction: 75% savings on textbook expenses
Access: 90% of students can afford subscription model
Collaboration: Cross-institutional knowledge sharing
Academic Success: Improved pass rates and engagement

Core Challenges

Financial Barriers: Textbook costs exclude many students
Knowledge Silos: Limited cross-institutional sharing
Digital Divide: Varying technology access levels
Academic Integrity: Plagiarism and content protection needs

STEP 2: RESEARCH

Understanding the South African higher education landscape and student needs

RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

Student Interviews

Participants: 45 students across 8 universities
Focus: Textbook acquisition strategies and financial struggles
Universities: WSU, UCT, Wits, UKZN, UP, UFS, NWU, UWC
Demographics: NSFAS recipients, middle-class, international students

Market Analysis

Financial Data: University fee structures and textbook costs
Competitor Analysis: International platforms (Cengage, Pearson)
NSFAS Research: Funding gaps and student allowances
Technology Adoption: Student device usage and connectivity

Behavioral Studies

Study Habits: How students currently access learning materials
Collaboration Patterns: Informal knowledge sharing networks
Technology Usage: WhatsApp groups, Facebook study communities
Academic Integrity: Understanding of plagiarism and copyright

KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS

Financial Impact Analysis

Textbook Cost Breakdown

New Textbooks: R2,500-R4,000 per subject

Annual Burden: R15,000-R20,000 for full course load

Used Market: 30-40% savings but limited availability

Digital Options: Often 20% cheaper but platform scattered

Library Access: Limited copies, high demand during exams

Student Coping Strategies

Current Workarounds

Photo Sharing: 78% share textbook pages via WhatsApp

Library Dependency: 65% rely heavily on library resources

Group Purchasing: 45% pool money to buy shared copies

Illegal Downloads: 32% admit to using pirated materials

Academic Impact: 56% report poor performance due to lack of materials

Cross-Institutional Learning Gaps

Knowledge Silos: Students at different universities studying same subjects lack communication channels | Resource Inequality: Rural universities have fewer library resources than urban institutions | Lecturer Accessibility: Limited interaction between students and lecturers from other institutions | Study Groups: 89% would join cross-university study groups if platform existed

Technology Adoption Patterns

Smartphone Usage: 94% have smartphones, 78% use for academic purposes

Data Constraints: 67% on limited data plans, prefer WiFi-dependent apps

App Preferences: WhatsApp (96%), Facebook (78%), YouTube (85%) for learning

Digital Literacy: High social media skills, moderate academic platform usage

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Core Platform Structure

  • Digital Library: Subscription-based e-textbook access
  • Study Groups: Cross-institutional collaboration spaces
  • Knowledge Sharing: Student-generated content and notes
  • Mentorship Hub: Graduate-undergraduate connections
  • Academic Integrity: Plagiarism detection and content protection

Subscription Model Design

  • Tiered Access: Basic (R99/month), Premium (R199/month), Institution (R149/student)
  • Content Library: Partnership with South African publishers
  • Offline Access: Download capabilities for data-conscious students
  • Multi-device Sync: Seamless access across phone, tablet, laptop

Community Features

  • Study Groups: Subject-based communities across universities
  • Q&A Forums: Peer-to-peer academic support
  • Expert Network: Access to lecturers and graduates
  • Resource Sharing: Student notes, summaries, and study guides
Business Model Strategy
  • B2C Subscriptions: Individual student monthly plans
  • B2B Partnerships: University-wide licensing agreements
  • Publisher Revenue Share: 60/40 split with content creators
  • Freemium Model: Basic access with premium features upgrade
  • NSFAS Integration: Approved vendor for textbook allowances
Technology Requirements
  • Mobile-First Design: Android and iOS native apps
  • Offline Capability: Content syncing for low-connectivity areas
  • Security Features: DRM protection, watermarking, access controls
  • Accessibility: Screen readers, text-to-speech, adjustable fonts
  • Analytics Platform: Usage tracking, engagement metrics, academic outcomes

KEY STRATEGIC LEARNINGS

Solving Real Problems Drives Adoption

Founder Experience: Personal struggles with textbook costs created authentic problem understanding.

Market Validation: 75% cost reduction addresses genuine student financial pain points, leading to high adoption rates.

Community-Driven Features Create Stickiness

Cross-Institutional Collaboration: Study groups across universities filled a gap students didn't know they had.

Network Effects: Value increases as more students join, creating natural growth momentum.

Academic Integrity Builds Trust

Platform Credibility: Built-in plagiarism detection and content protection gained university endorsements.

Publisher Partnerships: Strong DRM and anti-piracy measures enabled content licensing agreements.

Design for Educational Innovation

Accessibility First: Designing for financially disadvantaged students created a platform that benefits everyone • Mobile-First Strategy: Recognizing smartphone-dominant usage patterns in SA • Offline Capabilities: Addressing data cost concerns through downloadable content • Community Building: Technology as a catalyst for academic collaboration • Creative Innovation: Purple-branded design that inspires learning and creativity

Portfolio Conclusion

The Sesyme project demonstrates how user-centered design, combined with authentic problem understanding and social impact focus, can create transformative educational technology. By prioritizing accessibility, collaboration, and academic integrity, we've built a platform that not only serves students but actively improves educational outcomes across South African higher education.

Project Status Update: While the original Sesyme app was available on the Google Play Store, it appears to be no longer accessible, likely due to licensing challenges or business closure. This case study preserves the UX design process and innovative solutions developed for the educational technology sector.
