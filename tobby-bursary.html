01 · Tobby

Ask-IT chatbot that knows when to hand off

Tobby was built to deflect high-volume, repetitive IT queries — password resets, VPN access, software requests — into guided self-service. I owned the full UX: intent taxonomies, message patterns, fallback states, escalation rules, and UI states across web and mobile.

The goal wasn't pure automation. It was making the handoff to humans feel seamless when automation wasn't enough.

02 · Tobby process

From ticket data to conversation design

01

Top query types

Partnered with IT support to pull password, VPN, software access, and "how do I…?" categories as the first intent set.

02

Flow design

Happy paths + edge cases (missing info, wrong employee details, unsupported requests) with clear confirmations and fallbacks.

03

Personality & tone

Helpful and straightforward — short, actionable messages. Every line makes the next action obvious.

04

Escalation

Summarise conversation, capture missing details, create human ticket — employees never feel stuck in the bot.

03 · Bursary

Guided journey, not a silent PDF

The Vodacom External Bursary process had a drop-off problem: applicants didn't know if they were eligible, what documents they needed, or what happened after submit. I mapped the end-to-end service journey and redesigned it as a guided digital experience — with Tobby as the front door.

Now reading
04 · Bursary process

Eligibility → documents → submission → status

01

Journey mapping

Website, email, HR referrals, paper forms — unclear timelines, missing docs, no post-submit visibility.

02

Three-stage guided flow

Eligibility check, document preparation, submission — each with instructions, checklists, and time expectations.

03

Tobby as helper

Answers "Do I qualify?", "What documents?", "What's my status?" — then hands into the formal form only when ready.

04

Status communication

Received · under review · missing info · accepted · unsuccessful — via email and Tobby where appropriate.
